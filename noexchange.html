False equivalence: Palestinian refugees and Mizrahi Jews

The 'population exchange' myth

  • No bilateral agreement, no population transfer treaty, no UN resolution recognizes any 'exchange' â€“ the term itself is a political construct, not a legal reality.

Palestinian refugees: Forced expulsion

  • Plan Dalet (March 1948): Zionist leadership adopted a master plan to expel Palestinian civilians from areas designated for the Jewish state. At least 418 villages destroyed, 15 cities depopulated.
  • Massacres (Deir Yassin, Tantura, Lydda): Systematic violence and psychological warfare caused mass flight. Survivors were loaded onto trucks and expelled to the West Bank, Gaza, Jordan, Lebanon, and Syria.
  • UNGA Resolution 194 (1948): Affirms the right of Palestinian refugees 'to return to their homes and live at peace with their neighbors.' This right is individual, permanent, and non-waivable. It has been reaffirmed annually by the UN General Assembly.

Mizrahi immigration: Encouraged, not expelled

  • The Law of Return (1950) granted every Jew worldwide the right to immigrate to Israel and receive immediate citizenship. Mizrahi Jews were actively recruited, airlifted (Operation Ezra and Nehemiah, Operation Magic Carpet), and absorbed by the state.
  • Yehouda Shenhav, 'The Arab Jews' (2006): 'The narrative of Mizrahi refugees was constructed retroactively in the 1990s to counter Palestinian claims. At the time of their arrival, they were not considered refugees by Israel or the UN.'
  • UNHCR: Mizrahi Jews have never been registered as refugees, do not receive UNRWA or UNHCR assistance, and no UN resolution grants them a right of return to countries of origin.
